What about AI · XR · MR?

These technologies are emerging fast, and they will absolutely shape the next wave of immersive learning. They're outside this study's scope — but worth knowing where they stand.

Technology
● The Possibilities
● The Difficulties
AI
Artificial Intelligence
Adaptive, intelligent systems that personalise the learning experience.
Personalised pacing, instant feedback, and tutoring at scale. Could free teachers to focus on relational, deeper learning.
Concerns around bias, hallucination, student dependency, and ethical use. Schools still working out healthy boundaries.
XR
Extended Reality
An umbrella term covering AR, VR, MR — and everything in between.
A flexible framework for designing learning across the immersion spectrum, not locked into one device or modality.
More of a category than a tool. Hard to teach with directly until the underlying technologies mature individually.
MR
Mixed Reality
Digital content that interacts with physical surroundings in real time.
Best of AR and VR combined — place a virtual heart on the desk, walk around it, step inside, all without leaving the classroom.
Still emerging. Relies on advanced hardware like Microsoft HoloLens. Cost & access remain significant barriers.
!
Excluded from this study to maintain a clear, manageable scope at Master's level — but they are exactly where this work could grow next.
