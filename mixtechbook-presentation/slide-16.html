What I'll take with me.

Two formal learning outcomes anchor this project. Here's what each meant in practice — and what's stayed with me beyond the report.

Learning Outcome 1

Systematically plan, execute, complete and evaluate a work-based research project resulting in a substantial and original contribution to knowledge.

This project felt like the natural continuation of my Sri Lanka journey — pushing it further, asking a harder question: what does learning look like for the next generation? I tried to do too much. I learned what "ambitious" really means at Master's level — and how to make it work anyway.

planning methodology design project execution honest evaluation
Learning Outcome 2

Through engagement and reflective practice, extend knowledge and skills developing personal potential and professional competencies.

I came in knowing the broad potential of immersive tech. I leave with a much more nuanced and honest understanding — that the technology is not the answer, the design decisions surrounding it matter more than the tool itself, and adoption depends on conditions far beyond the hardware.

qualitative research thematic analysis VR design cross-cultural collaboration
