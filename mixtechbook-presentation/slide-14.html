What I'd build next.

This research has built a foundation — evidence, design directions, and a working prototype. Here's what should come after, in order of urgency.

01 Most urgent

Test it in a real classroom.

Bring the prototype into a Year 9 classroom and run structured trials with students. Move it from early stage prototype to evidence-based tool.

02 Voices missing

Put students at the centre.

Year 9 students didn't take part in this research. Future studies should involve them as active participants, not just users.

03 Validation

Curriculum & expert review.

Formal evaluation by curriculum specialists and ed-tech experts to fully align with Level 5 achievement objectives.

04 Build it properly

Realise all 5 design directions.

A complete prototype that fully addresses all five directions — including ethical review and learner wellbeing for the ~10% who can't use VR.

05 Core obligation

Embed mātauranga Māori.

In Aotearoa, this is not optional. Genuine collaboration with Māori educators and knowledge holders, from the earliest design stage.

References Dissanayake, N. L. (2026). Recommendations for Future Research, Chapter 11 — MixTechBook: The Future of Immersive Learning.
